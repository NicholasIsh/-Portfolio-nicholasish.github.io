Setting up Git:
I already had Git installed and set up on my computer from last year as I used it in group work and so I have some experience with it and know how to use it in terms of collaborations but have yet to explore its other features.

The Library of Babel is very interesting and has many similarities to the internet like its possession of limitless information (you just have to know where and how to find it). The chaotic organisation of the library is similar to the internet which is often seen as a chaotic and disorganised space where it can be difficult to find relevant information at times amidst the clutter of countless websites and pages. The redundancy of the library as it contains countless copies of the same books is similar to how the internet contains endless websites and articles covering the same topics. The librarians of the library are in a way holding a position of power as they are the gatekeepers of knowledge similar to the way search engines, social media platforms and governments can be seen as gatekeepers of information as they can through their algorithms and authority can suppress or boost information and narratives which shape and control information available to users which has effects upon the real world as people's ability to form their own opinions are removed without them even knowing.

My goal for this course is to learn new coding languages and utilise them to create websites built upon a solid foundation through the application of the theory we will learn this year.

Week 3

My experience learning CSS and continuing the development of your website:
This week we started to learn CSS and how it works with HTML inorder to give a website some more style and personality. I enjoyed learning about the different ways CSS can integrate with HTML, especially the way in which you can have a separate CSS script for styling the website as a whole. This method seems to be the easiest and cleanest way to go about integrating CSS into my website as instead of individually editing lines in my HTML files I can rather have the CSS styling applied throughout my entire website.With this new found knowledge I started working on integrating CSS into my website and experimenting with it. I focused primarily upon my home page as its style will be the template I will use to build the other pages within my website. I feel that I have grasped the concept of CSS and how to incorporate it into my website however I did struggle at first with it. I now am confident in my ability to make use of it.

Reflection on this week's reading:
Mailloux's article “Convention and Context”, emphasises the importance of considering the context in which the convention is being used as a design convention that works well in one context may not be appropriate in another. The reading has taught me that understanding conventions and context is crucial in designing user-centred digital products that are both functional and aesthetically pleasing. By being mindful of the conventions that users expect and the context in which they will use the product, it can help me to create digital experiences that are intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable to use.

Week 7

My experience continuing the development of my website:
This week I mostly worked on enhancing the user experience of my website by putting my finishing touches on the UI. in particular I have concentrated on polishing my portfolio and design pages. I also focused on fine tuning my wireframes and producing detailed design sketches that capture the full extent of my vision for the website.

My IxD Process during the development of my website:
My Goal: I want my website to impress users with its clean, elegant design and effectively communicate my skills as a web designer. I want users to be inspired by my work and motivated to contact me for design services or exploring more of my portfolio.

Information structure:
The website features a navigation bar at the top of each page for easy access. The home page introduces the creator and website. The blog page has a selection of blogs with a fixed navigation bar on the right for easy access, which remains visible while scrolling down the page. The design page displays a document in the centre left, detailing the website's design, with a similar navigation bar on the right. The profile page features a picture of the creator and information about their skills and interests, with a link to their portfolio page. The portfolio page has a similar layout to the design page, with a navigation bar on the right for easy navigation.

User Flow:
The website's home page is the starting point, with a top navigation bar to move to other pages. Blog, design, and portfolio pages have a similar layout, with a fixed navigation bar on the right side for easy access to specific sections. Users can scroll down to read more information on these pages. The profile page also has a scrollable layout and a button to access the portfolio page.

Reflection on this week's reading:
I found Hassenzahl and Tractinsky's paper "User Experience - A Research Agenda" to be a very insightful paper which focuses on the user experience. Hassenzahl and Tractinsky’s aim is to understand and improve user experience, emphasising the importance of understanding user needs, emotions, and behaviours. From the paper I have learnt the importance of considering the entire user experience, rather than just individual components. This means that as a website developer, I need to think about the user's experience from the moment they land on the website to the moment they leave. In developing my website I intend to keep the principles outlined in this paper in mind as they could be incredibly helpful and I can create a website that not only meets functional requirements but also provides a positive user experience. By considering the user's needs and emotions I can create a website that is not only functional but also enjoyable to use.

References:
Hassenzahl, M. & Tractinsky, N., 2006. User experience-a research agenda. Behaviour & information technology, 25(2), pp.91-97.
